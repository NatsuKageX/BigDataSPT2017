Call for Papers for the Second Annual Big Data Security, Privacy and Trust Workshop (BigDataSPT 2017)

Guangzhou, China
December 12-15, 2017

Call for Papers

 

Introduction

The volume, velocity and variety of data holdings are increasing, which demand cost-effective, efficient and innovative approaches or even a rethink in traditional approaches to ensure the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the information assets.This workshop aims to bring researchers, practitioners and other stakeholders together as a multi-disciplinary forum for discussion and dissemination of ideas to advance Big Data research.

 

Scope and Interests

Topics of interest comprise, but are limited to:

  • Research agendas that investigate big data security related issues (e.g. vulnerabilities on devices or technologies that can be exploited to compromise the confidentiality, integrity and availability of information assets).
  • Research agendas that identify big data privacy challenges, solutions and best practices (e.g. privacy-preserving in a complex big data environment, such as cloud and cyber-physical systems).
  • Research agendas that investigate big data trust related issues investigate
  • Papers that combine blue sky research and applied practice (e.g. cost effective retrieval, and analysis and evaluation of large data repositories).

Submission and Publication Information

Papers must be submitted electronically via the BigDataSPT 2017 submission page on the EasyChair website https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=bigdataspt2017. All papers need to be submitted electronically through the conference website in PDF format. Submitted papers must not substantially overlap with papers that have been published or that are simultaneously submitted to a journal or a conference with proceedings. Papers must be clearly presented in English, must not exceed 10 pages in Springer LNCS format(or up to 14 pages with the pages over length charge), including tables, figures, references and appendices. Papers will be selected based on their originality, significance, relevance, and clarity of presentation assessed by at least three reviewers. Submission of a paper should be regarded as a commitment that, should the paper be accepted, at least one of the authors will register and attend the conference to present the work. BigDataSPT 2017 reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference (e.g., removal from the digital library and indexing services), if the paper is not presented at the conference. All accepted papers will be published by Springer LNCS (EI Indexed).
